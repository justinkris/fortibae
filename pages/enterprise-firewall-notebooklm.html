Designing the Fortress From Scratch
L1 · ARCHITECTURE L3 · VLANs & VDOMs
You're two network security consultants who've just been handed a blank floor plan for a brand-new enterprise campus — headquarters, two data centres, and sixty branch offices. Your job: design the firewall architecture from scratch with zero budget constraints. Walk through why you'd place a Data Center Firewall at the network core, an Internal Segmentation Firewall between business units, and a Distributed Enterprise Firewall at every remote branch — mapping specific FortiGate hardware series to each location based on throughput needs. Then debate whether Zero Trust Network Access actually changes the deployment model or just rebrands existing concepts. Close by explaining how VLANs and VDOMs let a single FortiGate behave like multiple independent firewalls — use the analogy of apartments in a skyscraper versus building separate houses.
DCFW · ISFW · DEFW VDOMs Exam Weight: High

The Day the Firewall Died

Walk through how Active-Passive and Active-Active HA modes each respond to that moment of failure, debate which mode you'd choose for a hospital versus a video streaming platform, and break down the primary election algorithm step by step — ports monitored, uptime, priority, serial number — and why the order flips when override is turned on. End with the one CLI command that forces a failover for maintenance and the one situation where you'd actually use it.
FGCP A/P · A/A Primary Election Exam Weight: High

EPISODE · DOMAIN 1
The Smart Home That Fights Back
L9 · SECURITY FABRIC L10 · HW ACCELERATION
Imagine Fortinet's Security Fabric is an AI-powered smart home where every device — the thermostat, the cameras, the door locks — all talk to each other and act automatically when something goes wrong. Explain exactly what each chip offloads and what it can't touch.

EPISODE · DOMAIN 3
Reading Encrypted Mail Without Opening It
L6 · SSL/TLS INSPECTION L6 · WEB FILTER
A Fortune 500 employee just downloaded ransomware through an HTTPS link — and the firewall had absolutely no idea because the traffic was encrypted. Start there. Explain how SSL/TLS inspection works as a "man in the middle for good" — the firewall intercepts, decrypts, inspects, and re-encrypts traffic before it reaches the user. Walk through the full TLS handshake like it's a secret-agent handshake at a private club, explain why TLS 1.0 and 1.1 got kicked out of the club, and then tackle the curveball: HTTP/3 runs over QUIC over UDP — why does that break traditional inspection? Bring in the three inspection layers: Web Filter as the address cop (URL and domain only), Application Control as the behavioral detective (what is this app actually doing?), and ISDB as the address book that only knows IP and port. What can each see, and where does each fall short?
TLS 1.2 · 1.3 HTTP/3 · QUIC ISDB Exam Weight: High

EPISODE · DOMAIN 3
The Network's Immune System
L6 · IPS PROFILES
Compare the network's Intrusion Prevention System to the human immune system — it does nothing until it recognises a threat pattern, then it acts precisely and fast. Walk through how FortiGate IPS profiles work: the difference between filter type (category-based, like vaccinations for a class of viruses) and individual signatures (custom antibodies for a specific known strain), how severity levels and target (client vs server) narrow down which signatures matter for a given network, and what the action options — monitor, block, reset, quarantine — look like in practice. Then tell the story of hping3: a tool that sends crafted attack packets to test whether your IPS actually fires. Why would a defender run an attack simulator against their own firewall, and what result proves the IPS is working?
IPS Signatures hping3 Exam Weight: Medium

EPISODE · DOMAIN 4
Two Routers Walk Into a WAN
L5 · OSPF L5 · BGP
Frame this as a travel documentary. OSPF is the GPS that builds a complete map of every road in the city — it knows every intersection, every shortcut, and recalculates instantly when a road closes. Explain link-state routing in plain English, walk through Dijkstra's shortest path algorithm using a city map analogy, and introduce the three OSPF router types (Backbone, ABR, ASBR) as the city planner, the suburb connector, and the border crossing agent. Then BGP is the international postal network — it doesn't know road conditions, it knows who controls which postal district and negotiates the best routes based on policy, not just speed. Contrast IBGP (same company's internal postal offices) versus EBGP (agreements between different companies), explain AS path manipulation as "making your route look shorter on paper," and close with how ECMP lets you use both motorways simultaneously instead of picking just one.
OSPF · SPF BGP IBGP · EBGP ECMP Exam Weight: High

EPISODE · DOMAIN 4
The Art of the Translation Layer
L5 · REDISTRIBUTION L5 · FORTIMGR TEMPLATES
You're the network architect at a company that just acquired a smaller firm — they run OSPF internally, you run BGP, and nothing talks to anything. This is the redistribution problem. Explain why redistribution is disabled by default in FortiOS (a safety net against routing loops and route feedback) and walk through what it actually means to redistribute OSPF routes into BGP and vice versa — what information gets carried across and what gets lost in translation. Then tackle the loopback-as-BGP-source trick: why use a loopback interface instead of a physical port, how update-source and ebgp-enforce-multihop work together, and what breaks without them. Close by explaining how FortiManager routing templates turn a complex multi-router BGP deployment into a parameterised template you deploy once and push everywhere.
Redistribution Loopback BGP Source Exam Weight: Medium

EPISODE · DOMAIN 5
Fifty Safe Houses, One Network
L7 · IPsec IKEv2 L7 · DH · PFS · DPD
You're two intelligence officers designing secure communications between 50 field offices — and every conversation must be unbreakable even if a key is ever compromised. Walk through IPsec as the armoured vehicle for data: why it's the gold standard for site-to-site encryption, and how the five topologies (site-to-site, hub-spoke, dual hub, full mesh, ADVPN) are like choosing between a direct road, a star network of highways, a resilient dual-hub ring, a full mesh of private tunnels, or a smart network that builds shortcuts on demand. Deep-dive IKEv2's advantages over IKEv1 — AES-GCM, EAP authentication, stronger Diffie-Hellman groups. Explain Perfect Forward Secrecy as "burning the codebook after every single message." Then debate the three Dead Peer Detection modes — on-demand, on-idle, disabled — using the analogy of checking if a colleague is still at their desk.
IKEv2 PFS DPD Modes Exam Weight: High

EPISODE · DOMAIN 5
The Tunnel That Builds Itself
L8 · ADVPN L8 · SHORTCUT TUNNELS
You have 200 branch offices, all connected back to headquarters via a hub-and-spoke VPN — but now branch A needs to transfer a huge file to branch B, and every packet is making a detour through HQ first, saturating the hub. This is the problem ADVPN was built to solve. Walk through how ADVPN gives you hub-and-spoke management simplicity but full-mesh performance: the hub is the matchmaker, the spokes are the couples, and shortcut tunnels are direct connections that spring into existence the moment two branches need to talk directly. Explain the exact config that makes it work — why the hub needs type dynamic and auto-discovery-sender, why spokes need auto-discovery-receiver and net-device enabled, why IKEv2 is not optional here, and why static routes on the hub would completely break spoke subnet discovery. Close by walking through a FortiManager template that deploys ADVPN to all 200 branches using metadata variables for per-site addressing.
ADVPN Shortcut Tunnels FortiMgr Templates Exam Weight: High
